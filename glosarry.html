Solar Energy Glossary

1. Photovoltaic (PV) Cells

Definition: Photovoltaic cells, commonly known as solar cells, are devices that convert sunlight directly into electricity using the photovoltaic effect.

Usage: Photovoltaic cells are the building blocks of solar panels, capturing sunlight and generating electrical power for various applications.

2. Inverter

Definition: An inverter is a device that converts direct current (DC) electricity generated by solar panels into alternating current (AC) electricity used in homes and businesses.

Usage: Inverters play a crucial role in enabling the integration of solar power into the existing electrical grid, ensuring compatibility with standard appliances.

3. Net Metering

Definition: Net metering is a billing arrangement where excess electricity generated by a solar power system is fed back into the grid, and the customer receives credits on their electricity bill.

Usage: Net metering encourages the adoption of solar energy by providing financial incentives for users to contribute excess energy to the grid.

4. Kilowatt-Hour (kWh)

Definition: A kilowatt-hour is a unit of energy equivalent to one kilowatt (kW) of power expended for one hour.

Usage: Electricity consumption is often measured in kilowatt-hours, and it is a standard unit for billing by electric utilities.

5. Off-Grid System

Definition: An off-grid solar power system operates independently of the electrical grid, relying on energy storage solutions like batteries for power during periods of low sunlight.

Usage: Off-grid systems are commonly used in remote areas where connecting to the grid is impractical, providing sustainable energy solutions.
